The Cut We don't make clothes quickly. We make them properly. Here is the path a single jacket takes from your first visit to its final delivery.
i.

Consultation

An hour in the cutting room. We discuss occasion, posture, cloth and intent. No commitment, no charge.

— 60 minutes
ii.

Pattern & Baste

Twenty-two measurements taken. A paper pattern is drafted. Your first fitting is in a half-finished garment — held with white thread.

— Weeks 1 – 4
iii.

Forward Fitting

The garment is sewn down. We refine balance, sleeve pitch, and the line of the lapel. Small corrections, made by hand.

— Weeks 5 – 9
iv.

Finish & Delivery

Buttonholes cut, lining felled, edges pressed. We deliver in a cotton suit-bag — and we keep your pattern for life.

— Weeks 10 – 12
